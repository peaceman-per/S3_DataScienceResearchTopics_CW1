## Module
**MSc Data Science**  
**DSM060 – Data Science Research Topics**

## Assessment
**Midterm Coursework – Literature Review**  
**Topic Area:** Generative AI  
**Weighting:** 50% of final module mark

---

## Learning Objectives
The coursework is intended to help you:

- Reflect on research interests in Generative AI
- Identify specific research problems
- Evaluate suitability for an MSc Data Science project
- Analyse academic and grey literature
- Produce a structured, critical literature review

---

## Assignment Task

Select a **specific research question** within Generative AI and conduct a thorough literature review to:

1. Summarise key achievements related to the question
2. Examine methodologies used (strengths and limitations)
3. Identify gaps and open challenges
4. Discuss future research directions

Your review must be **analytical, critical, and well-structured**, supported by appropriate references.

---

## Recommended Sources
You are encouraged to draw from:
- Journal articles
- Conference proceedings
- Books
- Preprints (e.g. arXiv)
- Industry reports, blogs, and news articles

---

## Word Count
- **Maximum:** 3,000 words (excluding title page, figures, tables, references)
- **Recommended minimum:** 2,000 words

Penalties:
- Up to 10% over limit: −5 marks
- More than 10% over limit: mark of zero

---

## Required Structure (STRICT)

### 1. Title

### 2. Abstract (15 marks)
- High-level topic overview
- Problem description and relevance
- Review approach
- Key conclusions
- **≤ 500 words recommended**

### 3. Keywords (5 marks)
- Up to **four** distinctive keywords

### 4. Background (10 marks)
- Introduce topic area
- Importance and brief history
- Future trajectory
- Clearly state the research question

### 5. Review of the Literature (40 marks)
- Analyse **at least 10 sources**
- Use author–date citations (e.g. Smith (2023))
- Critically compare approaches
- Identify themes and contradictions
- Do **not** merely summarise papers

### 6. Conclusion and Discussion (20 marks)
- Synthesis of findings
- Achievements to date
- Limitations and causes
- Potential solutions
- Link to a possible future MSc project

### 7. Acknowledgements (optional, 0 marks)

### 8. References (10 marks)
- Minimum **10 references**
- Harvard style
- Alphabetically ordered

### 9. Optional Sections (0 marks)
- Personal reflection
- Appendices
- Attachments

---

## Academic Integrity
All sources must be cited.
Direct quotations must appear in quotation marks.
Plagiarism penalties are severe.
